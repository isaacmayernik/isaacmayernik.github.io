About me

My name is Isaac Mayernik. I am a Computer Science student at the University of South Carolina (graduating May 2025) working with a team to develop an Android app as my capstone project. Growing up in Greenville, SC, I developed a passion for problem-solving and technology that led me to pursue software development.

Outside of coding, I love working out, skiing, and kayaking. I also game and draw inspiration from Dark Souls, Super Metroid, and Bioshock - three great games that fuel my passion to create immersive experiences.

My goal is to develop engaging games that resonate with players, but I am also open to opportunities where I can apply my technical and creative skills.

Cocky's Way

Developed as a capstone project, Cocky's Way is an Android app using ReactJS that shows important locations around the UofSC Columbia campus. It also allows users to view all professors with relevant information, create a schedule based on courses currently available, and import BlackBoard settings to view assignment due dates.

View Website

Workout Log App

The Workout Log Android app was developed for the Android development course at UofSC using Kotlin and Jetpack Compose. It allows for easy logging of workouts and visualizes which muscle groups have been worked on.

View Website

CUHackit25

Participated in the Clemson University 2025 Hackathon (CUHackit25). Developed an Android app with Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, Python, and Python Flask that utilizes the AWS services provided during the event to create a prompt based on user entered information and generate a workout plan based on it.

Forgotten

Forgotten is a 3D Souls-like action-adventure game developed in UE5 using the blueprint system. The player controls a fox who must survive against enemies, defeat the golem boss, and reach the rest point.

View Itch.io Page

Frogbound

Frogbound is a 2D Metroidvania game developed in Unity using C#. The player controls a frog as they explore levels, unlock upgrades, and defeat enemies.

View Itch.io Page

Pocket Campus

Developed for a Software Engineering course, Pocket Campus was designed to be a system that would allow students to see which classes they still needed to complete their degree.
